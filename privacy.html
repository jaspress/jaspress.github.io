1. Overview

The SCP Foundation Institute of Anomalous Sciences ("the Institute") takes user privacy seriously. This Privacy Policy applies to the Journal of SCP Anomalous Sciences (JSAS) website and related online services. Please read this policy carefully to understand how we collect, use, and protect your personal information.

2. Information We Collect

The Institute may collect the following types of information:

  • Submitter Information: Name/anonymized ID, institutional affiliation, contact email, and manuscript content submitted via the submission form.
  • Contact Information: Name, email, and inquiry content provided via the contact form or direct email.
  • Access Logs: IP addresses, browser type, access time, and page paths (used for statistical analysis and security auditing).
  • Cookie Information: This site uses localStorage to save language preference settings. No third-party tracking cookies are used.

3. How We Use Information

Collected information is used only for the following purposes:

  • Processing manuscript submissions, peer review, and publication-related matters;
  • Responding to user inquiries and feedback;
  • Maintaining website security, tracking usage statistics, and improving user experience;
  • Displaying author information (anonymized ID or authorized real name) on published article pages with author consent;
  • Fulfilling legal obligations and Foundation internal security review requirements.

4. Information Sharing and Disclosure

The Institute does not sell, rent, or otherwise commercially provide your personal information to third parties. Exceptions include:

  • Peer Review: Manuscripts are sent anonymously to external reviewers with the author's informed consent (double-blind review);
  • DOI Registration: Metadata of published articles (title, authors, abstract) is submitted to CrossRef for DOI registration;
  • Legal Requirements: Disclosure may be required by law or Foundation O5 Council security directives.

5. Data Retention

Author information for published articles is retained permanently as part of the academic record. Author information for unpublished manuscripts is retained for 3 years after final processing, after which it is anonymized. Contact inquiry information is retained for 1 year after resolution.

6. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Request access to personal information the Institute holds about you;
  • Request correction of inaccurate personal information;
  • Request deletion of your personal information to the extent permitted by applicable law;
  • Withdraw previously given consent to information processing (without affecting processing carried out before withdrawal).

To exercise these rights, contact the Editorial Office: privacy@jsas.scp.org

7. Security Measures

The Institute uses industry-standard technical and administrative measures (including Foundation-specific anomalous information protection protocols) to protect your personal data against unauthorized access, disclosure, or tampering. Confidential reports are handled through end-to-end encrypted channels and are accessible only by the Editor-in-Chief and Ethics Committee Chair.

8. Policy Updates

The Institute reserves the right to revise this policy at any time. Significant changes will be announced via site notice and email to registered users. Continued use of this site constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
